Welcome

Learn about Stair Stalker a provocative app that allows us to learn about motion

Our Goal

Our project goal is to use signal filtering and frequency domain analysis to detect which set of stairs on Olin’s campus a person is using while maintaining the privacy of our user’s exact location.

Why this project?

The reason we decided to choose this topic is because it meets our learning objective to feasibly integrate the tools we learned about motion analysis while also allowing us to have room to challenge ourselves in the class.

There were also some valuable things we though our users would be able to get from the app,
which can be seen below:

Accessibility

Initially the reason we wanted to do this project was because we had personally experienced getting lost in this very complicatedly constructed school. We want to make Olin an less confusing experience for visitors and to find lost friends in “danger”.

Educational

We also understand that not everyone has the opportunity to learn higher math (motion equations and fourier analysis). This app could teach advanced mathematical skills that they would otherwise not be exposed to.

Mind Opening

Tied to Education, we wanted to get our audience to understand that these mathematical tools can be applied to our daily lives even through simple tasks like walking up and down the stairs.







Overview

For this project, we collected data through our user’s Smartphone and the MATLAB sensing application as they go up/down the stairs.

We compare a test data set to our stairwell frequency profiles and identify the closest location match.These frequency profiles were created by filtering our collected data and analyzing the filtered data in the frequency domain.
